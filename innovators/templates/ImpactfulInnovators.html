Meet The innovators

Cai Lun

ca. 50-121 CE

He is credited with the invention of paper and papermaking, which revolutionized communication, education, and literature

Johannes Gutenberg

1400-1468

He invented the movable type printing press, which enabled the mass production of books and the spread of knowledge

Leonardo da Vinci

1452-1519

He was a polymath who excelled in art, science, engineering, and invention. He created masterpieces such as the Mona Lisa and The Last Supper, and designed visionary machines such as the helicopter and the parachute


Isaac Newton

1642-1727

He was one of the greatest scientists of all time, who formulated the laws of motion and universal gravitation, invented calculus, and built the first reflecting telescope

Benjamin Franklin

1706-1790

He was a statesman, diplomat, author, printer, and inventor. He conducted experiments on electricity, invented the lightning rod and bifocals, and helped draft the Declaration of Independence

James Watt

1736-1819

He improved the steam engine, which was crucial for the Industrial Revolution. He also invented the concept of horsepower and the unit of power named after him

Louis Pasteur

1822-1895

He was a chemist and microbiologist who discovered the principles of vaccination, microbial fermentation, and pasteurization. He also developed vaccines for anthrax and rabies

Thomas Edison

1847-1931

He was a prolific inventor who held over 1,000 patents. He invented or improved devices such as the phonograph, the light bulb, the motion picture camera, and the electric power system

Alexander Graham Bell

1847-1922

He was an engineer and innovator who is best known for inventing the telephone. He also made contributions to fields such as acoustics, aviation, hydrofoils, and eugenics

Nikola Tesla

1856-1943

He was a genius inventor and engineer who pioneered the development of alternating current (AC) electricity, wireless communication, and radio. He also designed devices such as the Tesla coil, induction motor, and remote control

Marie Curie

1867-1934

She was a physicist and chemist who discovered radioactivity and two new elements: polonium and radium. She was the first woman to win a Nobel Prize and the only person to wi n two Nobel Prizes in different sciences

Albert Einstein

1879-1955

He was a theoretical physicist who developed the theory of relativity, one of the pillars of modern physics. He also proposed the concept of mass-energy equivalence (E=mc2) and won the Nobel Prize for his explanation of the photoelectric effect

Henry Ford

1863-1947

He was an industrialist and innovator who revolutionized the automobile industry with his assembly line production method and his affordable Model T car. He also founded the Ford Motor Company and became one of the richest men in history

Sigmund Freud

1856-1939

He was a neurologist and founder of psychoanalysis, a method of treating mental disorders by exploring the unconscious mind. He also introduced concepts such as the id, ego, superego, libido, Oedipus complex, and Freudian slip

Charles Darwin

1809-1882

He was a naturalist and biologist who formulated the theory of evolution by natural selection. He also wrote influential books such as On the Origin of Species and The Descent of Man3

Guglielmo Marconi

1874-1937

He was an engineer and inventor who pioneered wireless communication. He developed the first long-distance radio transmission system and won the Nobel Prize for his contribution to radio science

Michael Jackson

1958-2009

He was a singer, songwriter, dancer, and pop icon who is known as the King of Pop. He also revolutionized music videos with Thriller, broke racial barriers with MTV, and sold over 350 million records worldwide

Alexander Fleming

1881-1955

He was a physician and microbiologist who discovered penicillin, the first antibiotic, in 1928. He also shared the Nobel Prize for his discovery and its therapeutic effects

Steve Jobs

1955-2011

He was an entrepreneur and innovator who co-founded Apple Inc. and transformed the fields of personal computing, music, animation, and mobile devices. He also launched products such as the Macintosh, iPod, iPhone, iPad, and iTunes

Bill Gates

born 1955

He is a business magnate and philanthropist who co-founded Microsoft Corporation and became one of the richest people in the world. He also created software such as MS-DOS, Windows, Office, and Internet Explorer

Stephen Hawking

1942-2018

He was a physicist and cosmologist who made groundbreaking discoveries on black holes, quantum gravity, and the origin of the universe. He also wrote popular books such as A Brief History of Time and The Universe in a Nutshell

Martin Luther King Jr

1929-1968

He was a civil rights leader and Nobel Peace Prize laureate who advocated for nonviolent resistance against racial discrimination. He also delivered the famous "I Have a Dream" speech and led the March on Washington in 1963

Mahatma Gandhi

1869-1948

He was a political activist and leader of the Indian independence movement against British colonial rule. He also practiced and preached nonviolence, civil disobedience, and social reform

Mother Teresa

1910-1997

She was a Catholic nun and missionary who devoted her life to serving the poor and sick in India. She also founded the Missionaries of Charity, a religious order that operates in over 100 countries
